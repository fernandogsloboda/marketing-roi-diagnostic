# Marketing ROI & Customer Attribution Diagnostic 📊

## 📌 Project Overview
This project performs a deep-dive audit of a retail company's marketing performance. Using Python, I analyzed customer demographics, purchasing behavior, and campaign response rates to identify high-value segments and optimize marketing spend.

**Key Business Questions Addressed:**
* Which marketing campaigns deliver the highest ROI for different income tiers?
* How does education level correlate with Average Ticket Size?
* Which sales channels (Web, Store, Catalog) are preferred by high-ticket customers?
* What is the "Wallet Share" (percentage of income spent) across customer segments?

## 🛠️ Tech Stack & Tools
* **Language:** Python 3.x
* **Libraries:** Pandas (Data Manipulation), NumPy (Numerical Analysis), Matplotlib/Seaborn (Visualization).
* **Environment:** Jupyter Notebook.

## 🚀 Key Insights & Results
* **Campaign Optimization:** Campaign 5 achieved a **27.5% conversion rate** among High-Income earners, while being completely ineffective for lower-income tiers.
* **Omnichannel Behavior:** High-value customers are not just "web-based"; they show a strong preference for **Catalog and In-Store** purchases.
* **Education vs. Spending:** Postgraduate customers exhibit a **48% higher LTV (Lifetime Value)** compared to Undergraduates.
* **Wallet Share:** Contrary to expectations, high-income segments allocate a larger portion of their annual earnings to this specific retailer, indicating high brand loyalty.
